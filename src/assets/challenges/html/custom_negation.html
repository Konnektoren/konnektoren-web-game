Negation in German: Kein vs. Nicht

1. Ich habe _______ Hund. (I don't have a dog.)

Use 'kein' to negate a noun with no article (e.g., 'Hund' - dog).

2. Wir haben _______ Zeit. (We have no time.)

Use 'kein' to negate a noun with no article (e.g., 'Zeit' - time).

3. Er arbeitet _______ am Wochenende. (He doesn’t work on weekends.)

Use 'nicht' to negate a verb or an adverbial phrase (e.g., 'arbeitet am Wochenende' - works on weekends).

4. Das ist _______ mein Buch. (That is not my book.)

Use 'nicht' to negate a noun phrase with an article (e.g., 'mein Buch' - my book).

5. Der Film war _______ interessant. (The movie wasn’t interesting.)

Use 'nicht' to negate adjectives (e.g., 'interessant' - interesting).
