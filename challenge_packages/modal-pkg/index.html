Modal Verbs in German

Modal verbs are special verbs in German that modify the meaning of the main verb in the sentence. The six main modal verbs in German are:

  • können – can, to be able to
  • müssen – must, to have to
  • wollen – to want
  • sollen – should, to be supposed to
  • dürfen – may, to be allowed to
  • mögen – to like

Modal verbs are usually placed in the second position of a sentence, and the main verb moves to the end of the sentence in its infinitive form.

Example:

Ich kann gut schwimmen. (I can swim well.)

Modal Verb Conjugations

Modal verbs in German are irregular. Let's look at the conjugation of können (to be able to) as an example:

Conjugation of "können":

Ich kann

Du kannst

Er/Sie/Es kann

Wir können

Ihr könnt

Sie können
